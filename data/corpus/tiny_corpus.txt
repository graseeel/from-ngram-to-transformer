The lantern keeper wrote every weather note in the margin of an old atlas.
At sunrise, the harbor sounded like glass bells and distant engines.
When the tide changed, children counted gulls and named the clouds.
The apprentice learned that small patterns often announce large storms.
She copied the sequence once, then twice, then asked what came next.
The old model guessed from memory; the new model listened to context.
Neither model knew the sea, but both learned traces left by language.
